INTRODUCTION

This study examines the Bible teaching on the subject of hellfire, including events leading up to it, its duration, and then the end of it! The purpose of the study is to reveal that, although the Hell experience will not be a pleasant one, the amount of suffering each person endures will be determined by the extent of his or her deeds in opposition to God’s will while on this earth! Jesus Himself declared:

“And, behold, I come quickly; and my reward is with me, to give every man according as his work shall be.” Revelation 22:12.

Why must we know the truth about this “Ever-Burning Hell” question? There are a number of important reasons:

  1. Because we need to know what all truth is, so that we’re not deceived.

  1. Because millions of folks have been “turned off” from religion by the mistaken idea that God is maliciously going to continually torment those who dare to reject Him.

  1. Further, some people don’t realize the necessity for GOD to put a stop to evil at some point, in order to protect the universe(s). An “ever-burning Hell” would perpetuate evil by reminding the inhabitants of this and other worlds of it.

  1. That this cleansing by fire is actually an act of mercy, due to God’s great heart of incredible LOVE! It rescues those who have been subjected to the evil that has surrounded them, while putting those who are irrevocably embroiled in the misery of sin, by (or through) their own choosing, out of their misery!

Note: GOD only requires us to take action when it is for our best good!

If you use a different Bible version, you won’t be able to use this concordance to discover the meanings of many Bible words.

That, in addition to the above reason, is why we are using the KJV. Also, the reader might do well to find older concordances, because publishers have been, and are, making changes in the newer versions in order to accommodate modern religious thinking, and are making new source materials that harmonize with these new beliefs.

Word Interpretations and Meanings

To start our study, let us look at the words that translators had to work with in the original languages—Hebrew in the Old Testament, and Greek in the New Testament. Let’s also give them a little slack, realizing that meanings of words have changed over the centuries.

We are looking first at the Hebrew and Greek words from which the word “Hell” has been translated. We will discover that the meanings are different from our modern English definitions. From there we will proceed to the three Hebrew words that are translated “forever” and their meanings. In the chart that follows, we will read actual verses that contain the word “forever,” or, “for ever,” from the Old Testament, in order to determine if those usages make sense in the context of the verses themselves. Afterward, we will look at the same information in the New Testament using the Greek words.

Perhaps the reader is wondering what the purpose of the word exercise below is. That purpose is this: It will show that the translators had a variety of words to use. What they wound up using was dependant on meanings current in their time, as well as in some cases, perhaps, filtering the words through their own beliefs. Our desire is to compare the original word meanings with our own present day meanings. We also want to approach the study with an open mind, asking for the guidance of the Holy Spirit as promised in His Word.

“Howbeit when he, the Spirit of truth, is come, he will guide you into all truth: for he shall not speak of himself; but whatsoever he shall hear, that shall he speak: and he will shew you things to come.” John 16:13.

The Old Testament Word for “Hell”

The only Hebrew word translated “Hell” is sheol (sheh-ole’). Although sheol appears 65 times in the Old Testament, it is only translated “hell” in 31 of those places, four of which have a marginal reading of “the grave.” It is also translated “grave” 31 times, but if you include the four marginal renderings, it would total 35 times. It is three times translated “pit,” but in each of those, the grave or hole in the earth is obviously meant. Where it has been translated “hell,” in the great majority of cases the word “grave” would have been just as plausible.

Sheol is the destiny of all mankind, not just the wicked. It was where Jacob and Job expected to go.

“Whatsoever thy hand findeth to do, do it with thy might; for there is no work, nor device, nor knowledge, nor wisdom, in the grave [sheol], whither thou goest.” Ecclesiastes 9:10.

“And all his sons and all his daughters rose up to comfort him; but he refused to be comforted; and he said, For I will go down into the grave [sheol] unto my son mourning. Thus his father wept for him.” Genesis 37:35.

“O that thou wouldest hide me in the grave [sheol], that thou wouldest keep me secret, until thy wrath be past, that thou wouldest appoint me a set time, and remember me!” Job 14:13.

The New Testament Words for “Hell”

There are three words translated “Hell” in the New Testament. Below are the words and meanings.

  1. Geenna (Gheh’-en-nah)—a small valley just outside the city of Jerusalem, used as the city dump!

Note: Due to the heat produced by gasses from the decaying material, and the destruction by burning down of material in order to make room for more dumping, there is a continual smoke rising from the site! Jesus uses this in a figurative sense, because of the smell, maggots and worms, it created a very distasteful image in the minds of the people! (See Matthew 5:22 for one such use.)

  1. Hades (Hah’-dace, but generally pronounced Hay’deez)— the unseen place or state of the dead! (See Matthew 11:23 and Revelation 20:14 for such use.) Note that in the latter verse, we see that it is both Death and Hell that are destroyed in the fire at the end of the world!

  1. Tartaroo (Tar-tar-o’-o)—The deepest abyss of Hades (see its only occurrence in 2 Peter 2:4). If this is a place of burning and torment where the fallen angels are imprisoned, someone must have left the keys in the door, for evil angels (demons or devils) are running rampant through the earth. The word must refer to our fallen planet where Satan and his hosts are quarantined from the rest of the universe. Eventually this earth will melt with fervent heat (2 Peter 3:10, 12).

The New Testament Words for “For Ever”

Forever is divided into two words, for and ever. The Greek word for “for” is eis (pronounced “ice”). It is defined as: “to or into (indicating the point reached or entered).”

Often the Greek word for “ever” is aion (pronounced ahee-ohn’), and it is defined as: “properly, an age; by extension, perpetuity (also past); by implication, the world.”

So the correct meaning of the two words together is “to a point reached in the ages.” Depending on the context of the verse, the exact intent of the writer must be determined. Here are some verses with these words:

“And lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from evil: For thine is the kingdom, and the power, and the glory, for ever. Amen.” Matthew 6:13.

“To whom be glory for ever and ever [eis aion aion], Amen.” Galatians 1:5.

“And the smoke of their torment ascendeth up for ever and ever [eis aion aion] : and they have no rest day nor night, who worship the beast and his image, and whosoever receiveth the mark of his name.” Revelation 14:11.

The expression “for ever and ever” in the two texts above is translated from the same Greek words, “eis” and “aion”, with the “aion” being repeated; in other words, “eis aion aion.” Bible writers commonly repeated words to add emphasis or importance. For examples, see Isaiah 6:3 and Revelation 4:8 where we find “Holy, Holy, Holy.”

“For perhaps he therefore departed for a season, that thou shouldest receive him for ever.” Philemon 1:15.

This raises a question: Did Paul expect Philemon, the owner of the slave, Onesimus, to keep him as a slave throughout eternity? Obviously, “the point reached at in the ages” would be, at most, the lifetime of either of the two individuals!

“But this man, [Jesus] after he had offered one sacrifice for sins for ever, sat down on the right hand of God;” Hebrews 10:12.

This last verse has a new word following “eis”; it is from the Greek word “dienekes” (pronounced dee-ay-nek-es’), meaning: “carried through, perpetually, continually, for ever.”

This combination gives us the meaning that we have always understood for the expression “for ever”! The next texts also comes from “eis dienekes.”

“From henceforth expecting till his enemies be made his footstool. For by one offering he hath perfected for ever them that are sanctified.” Hebrews 10:13-14.

“Jesus Christ the same yesterday, and to day, and for ever [eis aion].” Hebrews 13:8.

Hell-Fire and its Results

Now, let’s begin to add all these things together in order to develop in our minds what God is telling us about a supposedly ever-burning hell!

Here, in what is arguably the most famous Bible text there is, we find an amazing clue!

“For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life.” John 3:16.

Let’s break this text down to grasp the important details. What is it that God did and what was the motivation? “For God so loved the world that he gave his only begotten Son…”

And what was the purpose of this great gift? So that “whosoever believeth in him should not perish!” Should not what? Perish! Does “perish” mean to continue to live? According to Strong’s Concordance, the Greek word from which we get “perish” is apollumi (pronounced ap-ol’-loo-mee).

The word means “to destroy fully.”

If anyone is to “burn forever,” in order for that to happen and for it to be any kind of punishment, he or she would have to continue living throughout the period of time that the fire was burning, correct? If so, and if the theory of an “ever-burning Hell” is also correct, that would mean that those burning forever would need to have immortality, the ability to not die!

What does the Bible say about immortality?

“Which in his times he shall shew, who is the blessed and only Potentate, the King of kings, and Lord of lords; Who only hath immortality, dwelling in the light which no man can approach unto; whom no man hath seen, nor can see: to whom be honour and power everlasting. Amen.” 1 Timothy 6:15,16.

The Greek word rendered immortality is athanasia (ath-an-as-ee’-ah). It means deathlessness, which is the inability to die!

Returning to John 3, the very next verse says:

“For God sent not his Son into the world to condemn the world; but that the world through Him might be saved.” John 3:17.

How marvelous the love of God is!

Please note that without the sacrifice of Christ we are all condemned, because we are all sinners!

“For the wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord.” Romans 6:23.

In other words, we have earned death!

And without this fabulous, FREE offering of His precious life, as payment for our sins, we would ALL PERISH! (Or maybe, we would have never come into existence!) We must have faith in that offering!

Now, here is the passage that is most often used to buttress the idea of an unquenchable, or ever-burning fire. By the way, “unquenchable” means that it cannot be put out; it must go out by itself! Many firemen have battled with unquenchable fires. When a home burns to the ground, it is because the fire was unquenchable and did not go out until the fuel was consumed.

REVELATION 14:9-11

“And the third angel followed them, saying with a loud voice, If any man worship the beast and his image, and receive his mark in his forehead, or in his hand, The same shall drink of the wine of the wrath of God, which is poured out without mixture into the cup of his indignation; and he shall be tormented with fire and brimstone in the presence of the holy angels, and in the presence of the Lamb: And the smoke of their torment ascendeth up for ever and ever: and they have no rest day nor night, who worship the beast and his image, and whosoever receiveth the mark of his name.” Revelation 14:9-11.

Consider this!

“...and he shall be tormented with fire and brimstone in the presence of the holy angels, and in the presence of the Lamb:” Rev 14:10.

If we are to believe that this is literal, then we must believe that all the Holy angels are trapped or imprisoned, bound there for all eternity, detained in order to be in the presence of that eternal burning, if it were, in fact, to be eternal burning! God may be omnipresent, but not the angels.

Here’s an exercise for the reader: Take a wooden kitchen match and light it! Hold it with the head at a slightly higher level and when the head has stopped burning, wet your thumb and forefinger of your other hand. Now hold the match by the head, and allow the rest of the match to burn.

Did you quench it or did it go out by itself because there wasn’t any more material capable of sustaining the fire? This test illustrates the meaning of the phrase “everlasting fire”! It lasts until there is nothing left that is burnable!

QUESTION: What is the context of the above passage?

ANSWER: John is in vision, and describing what he sees in vision. We don’t know how long the vision lasted, but it was of course, a limited period of time! The vision and language obviously symbolize a future event! It was condensed! Does John continue to see it for countless eons to come? Is he still watching today? Did he envision that the fire would always burn? Or, is this a misunderstanding on our part because of the wording? Let’s ask ourselves a few questions relating to this passage.

First, what does drinking of the “wine of the wrath of God” mean? It means God is recompensing those who have hurt

His children and desecrated His universe! The wicked are partaking of God’s wrath.

Secondly, what does “without mixture” mean? It means that the wine of wrath is undiluted! Up until now God’s judgments have been mixed with mercy, but now mercy has ceased.

Thirdly, does their “torment” (burning) last forever? If this was so, that would mean that these people would have immortality, the inability to die, which would contradict what we found in 1 Timothy 6:15, 16! It is the smoke from the fire that is described as rising up “for ever and ever”! Most everyone has stood next to a campfire that was slowly burning out. The smoke rising up into the air, ever onward and upward, appears to rise forever until out of sight. Eventually, however, no more smoke will rise from the ashes. Why? Because the fuel is consumed.

Fourthly, would a continual burning (such as seems to be described) be fair? We know that God is fair, so would this be in harmony with His character of eternal love?

“Shall not the Judge of all the earth do right?” Genesis 12:8. Note what God said to the prophet Ezekiel:

“Say unto them,[the Children of Israel, and us] As I live, saith the Lord GOD, I have no pleasure in the death of the wicked; but that the wicked turn from his way and live: turn ye, turn ye from your evil ways; for why will ye die, O house of Israel?” Ezekiel 33:11.

Can we hear and weep with Him, thinking of the pathos in His plea? God has described His own mindset in the Bible in many places.

“For I am the LORD, I change not; therefore ye sons of Jacob are not consumed.” Malachi 3:6.

Would it be equitable for a person who was born a few years before the end of the world, having rejected God’s calling, to suffer the punishment as long as do Adolph Hitler, Cain or Satan? Even worse, should one who has rejected God, but otherwise was a relatively decent person, who lived 5,000 years ago, have to suffer 5,000 years longer than Hitler or Stalin? How do you answer these questions?

Fifthly, imagine you were in Heaven. Would you like to be able to check in on Uncle George, Mom, Dad, or your children occasionally, throughout all eternity, to see how much of them has burned, or to just listen to their screams? If you could do that, would it be heaven for you? Do you think our loving God would care to do that? If this were the case, the Omniscient God Himself would have to see all His children that He loves passionately, suffering…for eternity! Can you imagine that?

Lastly, does the idea of “ever-burning hell” agree with the rest of the Scriptures? Well, let’s do some investigation and see what we find.

End Time Events (Part One)

Here’s what the Bible says happens at the coming first resurrection:

“For the Lord Himself shall descend from heaven with a shout, with the voice of the archangel, and with the trump of God: and the dead in Christ shall rise first: Then we which are alive and remain shall be caught up together with them in the clouds, to meet the Lord in the air: and so shall we ever be with the Lord.” 1 Thessalonians 4:16-17.

Note that the phrase, “shall we ever” in verse 17 is from the Greek word pantote (pan’-tot-eh), meaning, “at all times.” This is made possible because they “put on immortality” at the resurrection (1 Corinthians 15:52-54).

By the way, the “shout” and the “voice” of the archangel disprove the “secret rapture” theory! Also, regarding this so-called “secret rapture,” how does it line up with Revelation 1:7, which says, “Behold, he cometh with clouds; and every eye shall see him, and they also which pierced him…”?

What, in addition to faith in Jesus, qualifies God’s people to be raised in this first resurrection? There is no such thing as “faith alone.” To say that I believe, and to take no action on God’s word, indicates that I don’t believe! According to James, “Faith without works is dead” (James 2:20). So, faith, for it to be real faith, requires us to take action! What kind of action in particular? The following texts will help answer this question.

“And the dragon was wroth [orgizo, pronounced or-gid’-zo, meaning enraged] with the woman, and went to make war with the remnant of her seed, which keep the commandments of God, and have the testimony of Jesus Christ.” Revelation 12:17.

NOTE: The scriptures symbolize a church as a woman! Either a good or evil church, depending on their deeds! (See the Prophetic Symbols Chart, pg. 41.)

“Here is the patience of the saints: here are they that keep the commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus.” Revelation 14:12.

  1. She not only accomplishes her purpose through teaching, but through legislation. Her fornication is with “the kings of the earth” and through them she is able to coerce the earth’s inhabitants.
